# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Homeowners and business owners in the Región de O'Higgins, Chile, who are actively seeking security solutions. They are typically:
- Homeowners in peri-urban or semi-rural areas (parcelas, condominios) who have experienced or fear burglaries
- Small-to-medium business owners (comercios, bodegas, talleres) who need to protect inventory and assets
- Industrial facility managers looking for certified monitoring (OS-10) with rapid response
- They arrive anxious about crime statistics and need to feel they're making the right, technically sound decision — fast

## Product Purpose

Generate qualified leads for Pro Espía's monitoring services while establishing the company as the most technologically advanced security operation center in the region. The site must convert visitors into contacts (form submissions, WhatsApp clicks, phone calls) while simultaneously building enough technical credibility that the visitor trusts the service before reaching out. Both goals are equally important — conversion without trust produces low-quality leads; trust without conversion produces no revenue.

## Brand Personality

Modern, aggressive, technological. The feel should be that of an active surveillance command center — precise, alert, always-on. Think control room dashboards, tactical interfaces, real-time telemetry. Not warm and fuzzy, not cold and military. The aesthetic is "professional urgency" — the visitor should sense that behind the site there's a real operations center staffed 24/7.

## Anti-references

- **Paramilitary / too bellicose**: No camouflage, no heavy-handed military imagery, no "war on crime" rhetoric that alienates homeowners. The technology protects; it doesn't assault.
- **Traditional alarm company clichés**: Generic clipart of broken windows, cartoon burglars, overly literal security imagery, cold blue/gray corporate templates. Pro Espía is modern, not the "alarma clásica" they're trying to replace.

## Design Principles

1. **Command-center confidence, not alarm-company fear.** The dominant feeling should be control and capability, not panic. Every visual choice reinforces "we see everything, we respond instantly" — not "you're in danger."
2. **Technical precision before decoration.** The dark navy palette, monospace labels, and grid overlays exist because control rooms look this way, not because "dark mode is cool." Form follows function.
3. **One call to action per viewport.** The page must always make it obvious what to do next. Contact forms, WhatsApp buttons, and phone numbers are never hidden. Every section that builds credibility must immediately offer a conversion path.
4. **Local authority through specific detail.** Rancagua, Machalí, Rengo, San Fernando, Pichilemu — the site names streets, comunas, response times, certification numbers. Generic security marketing talks about "advanced technology"; Pro Espía talks about what happens on the ground in O'Higgins.
5. **Motion with purpose, not decoration.** Scan lines, pulsing badges, telemetry-style states — each animation communicates "active, live, monitoring." If it doesn't signal operational status, it doesn't move.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Dark theme must maintain minimum 4.5:1 contrast for body text and 3:1 for large text against the navy background. Placeholders and muted labels must also meet 4.5:1. Reduced motion alternatives required for all animations.
